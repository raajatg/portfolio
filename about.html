Hi, I’m Raajat. I use design as a tool for building communities.

Why can’t we be friends?

Can you count how often you’ve heard that we live “in unprecedented times” just in the last 6 months? Maybe you could do without seeing the term as the opening line in every email you’ve read, but the truth is that 2020 has exposed some unfortunate truths about our society – social distancing due to COVID has made it clear just how isolated many of us are, and terrible police brutality has forced us to confront the massive divides and discrimination that still plague our institutions. I believe strong design is a way for us to reverse this trend of divide and disconnect. Design elevates the value we provide to your users, and deliver meaning with the experiences we create. I’ve consistently found that creating a sense of community and oneness with the experiences I build has helped me build the most meaningful experiences. I’ve also found that to build that sense of community, it is important to look beyond individual interaction experiences. It’s essential to understand the connections between multiple stakeholders, the ecosystem they operate in, and the existing opportunities and limitations in creating sustainable bonds within that ecosystem. In short, compelling community design requires a strategic eye. With past careers in management consulting and entrepreneurship, I can identify overarching opportunities for widespread impact, and translate those opportunities into benefits for all impacted stakeholders. My work as a strategy consultant refined my analytical and structuring skills, so that I’m able to easily identify community-level opportunities from individual user research. My experience starting various non-profits and social ventures taught me firsthand how to build community-driven models that drive value for users, corporations, and third parties, shedding the stereotype that doing good is incompatible with being profitable. In short, I combine a deep understanding of UX design and research skills with sharp analytical and strategic capabilities. This kaleidoscope of skillsets has helped me build solutions that benefit individuals by connecting them to one another and achieve mutually benefical goals. More importantly, I’ve also been able to find new and exciting answers to one simple question:

Why can’t we be friends?
